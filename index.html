Cover Letter: Why Solve?

A summary of my interest in Solve, my vision for program curation and why I can contribute to the broader team.

Submitted to MIT Solve Team June 07, 2017 Reach Out to Jacob
Cover Letter
  • Cover Letter

Why Work on Solve?

I’m drawn to your team because I believe your model’s commitment to connecting ideas to resources will actually solve problems. Solve and the broader MIT network bring a compelling mix of relevant expertise, assets and influence that can build a marketplace full of both solvers and motivated funders like few others – you’re well-positioned to make this work.

I’m looking for a role that will harness the expertise, connections and motivation of your communities to make an impact. To date, I've spent my career in an industry that uses its data, assets and influence to optimize the sales of goods and services, and I’ve been given several opportunities to learn and advance at my current employer. That said, I'm intent on refocusing my time to projects that prioritize creating social utility rather than solely profit. Solve strikes me as an incredible way to do that.

Impact of Program Curator

Solve’s programming and content orbits around two major events during each year, Solve at MIT in particular. As described, the Program Curator’s major function is to coordinate the definition, build and delivery of content for these events.

With that as the foundation, I believe that growing membership in communities and the engagement within them should also be a priority for this role at Solve – particularly beyond the reach of major events. As the organization evolves, I expect that the fixed timeline from competition launch to pitch event will be complemented by always-on challenges, impromptu partnerships and ongoing community-centric programming for health, education, economic prosperity and sustainability.

My hope is that program curation evolves into a shared function whose main purpose is to channel the expertise and efforts of the Solve community into content that feeds events, reinforces community development and enhances marketing.

Why I Can Contribute

I believe my capabilities to manage projects, content and stakeholders across diverse backgrounds, cultures and industries are highly transferable to program curation at Solve.

In my recent role as China Program Manager at LoyaltyOne, I led subject-matter experts to translate our business’ IP into content that would guide our start-up partner through the launch of their business.

  • I was the central point of contact between technical teams, subject matter experts, service providers, executives and stakeholders in Toronto and Beijing
  • I worked alongside our experts in technology, BD, analytics and marketing to build and deliver research, processes, financial models, presentation pitches and system requirements to our partners in China
  • I built and implemented agile processes for team collaboration internally and with partners to ensure we aligned on short-term sprint goals while remaining flexible to constantly changing priorities

My recent interest in machine learning has also spawned a project in content curation called AI From Scratch, gathering learning resources for people with non-STEM backgrounds. Previous to this, I’ve also been a content creator myself running restaurant review blogs in Toronto and New York.

Further, I believe growing organizations need flexible team members that aren’t limited by their function and are willing to evolve outside their lane. My current role demanded more than project management, including work on business development, partnerships, client management, financial modeling, and my previous roles focused on B2B digital and content marketing. I would welcome the chance to develop and apply this broader capability set in support of the team.

If I match your needs for this role or other opportunities at Solve, I’d love to discuss more about our mutual fit.

Best,
Jacob Younan
